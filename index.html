Privacy Policy

Last updated: 28 June 2026

Horbit is designed to be private by default. This policy explains, in plain language, what the app does and does not do with your data.

Short version

  • All your data stays on your device. Tasks, schedule, streaks, projects and settings are stored locally on your phone.
  • No account, no sign-up, no login. We never ask for an email or any identifier (beyond the optional first name you type for the greeting).
  • No servers, no cloud, no analytics, no tracking, no ads. The app does not send your data anywhere.
  • Works fully offline. Fonts and icons are bundled into the app.

What data the app stores (locally only)

The following is saved only on your device and is never transmitted:

  • Tasks you create, their categories, frequencies and durations.
  • Your daily completion logs and streak history.
  • Your weekly schedule (blocked time such as sleep/work) used to compute free time.
  • Projects you create.
  • App settings, including your optional first name and reminder time.

You can delete this data at any time through the app, by clearing the app's storage in your system settings, or by uninstalling the app. Uninstalling removes all of it permanently.

Notifications

If you enable the daily reminder, the app schedules a local notification on your device at the time you choose. This is handled entirely by your operating system; no reminder data leaves your phone.

Backup / export

The app lets you manually export your data as text so you can copy it somewhere safe and re-import it later. This export happens only when you choose to do it, and the resulting text goes wherever you paste it. The app itself does not upload it anywhere.

Children's privacy

Horbit does not knowingly collect any personal data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If the app ever changes how it handles data, this policy will be updated and the date at the top will change accordingly.
